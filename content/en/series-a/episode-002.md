## The First Step of School, Family, and Community Collaboration

On January 30, 2026, we held a workshop titled "Empowering Adults to Unlock Children's Potential" for parents during the open school day at Furugen Junior High School in Yomitan Village.

Despite being a weekday morning, 21 parents and school officials gathered, creating an atmosphere full of enthusiasm. This report documents what we shared that day and the future we aim to build together.

## Furugen JHS's Strength Seen Through Data

One of the most striking parts of the workshop was the data shared by the Vice Principal. The "well-being" (happiness) level of Furugen JHS students significantly exceeds the national average.

This is not just about "having fun." It demonstrates that the collaborative system where the school, community, and parents work together to support children directly links to the students' mental fulfillment.

## Cultivating the "Meaning of Learning"

We also shared an episode from this semester's opening ceremony when a professional wrestler visited the school. In response to the question "Why do we study?", we reaffirmed the importance of fostering "intrinsic motivation" not just through textbooks but through real-world voices from society.

## Preparing for the Future: Multiple Intelligences (MI Theory)

In the second half, we introduced Howard Gardner's "Theory of Multiple Intelligences."
Instead of a single yardstick like "Can they study or not?", this perspective shines a light on the eight diverse intelligences children possess: Linguistic, Logical-Mathematical, Musical, Bodily-Kinesthetic, Spatial, Interpersonal, Intrapersonal, and Naturalist.

Feedback from participating parents included:
- "It gave me a chance to understand the inner thoughts of my child during their rebellious phase."
- "It was a precious opportunity to talk with other parents."

## Conclusion

Education does not end within the school walls. When adults learn, connect, and change their perspectives, the potential of children becomes limitless.

I am confident that this step at Furugen Junior High School marks the beginning of a "circulation" that nurtures children through the entire community.
